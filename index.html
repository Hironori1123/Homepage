Hiro(nori) Katsuda

PhD candidate, UCLA Department of Linguistics

I am a fifth year linguistics graduate student at the University of California, Los Angeles and a member of the UCLA Phonetics Lab and the UCLA Language Acquisition Lab. My primary research interests lie in theoretical and experimental phonology, prosody, and fieldwork.

I am currently writing my dissertation on loanword accentuation in Japanese, which involves corpus analysis, computational modeling and experimentation.

Recent updates

  • I'll be presenting a talk at the 95th annual meeting of Linguistic Society of America. The title is "A probabilistic account of accentuation across Japanese lexical strata"
