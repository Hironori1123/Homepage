Hiro(nori) Katsuda

Visiting Assistant Professor
University of Kansas, Department of Linguistics

I am a phonologist specializing in the lexicon-phonology interface and phonology in language contact, including loanword phonology and bilingual phonology. I also work as a phonetician, exploring the effects of prosody, speaking rate, and sociolinguistic factors, on the production and perception of speech sounds. In summer 2024, I became a Visiting Assistant Professor in phonology at the University of Kansas. Before this, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Toronto Scarborough with Professor Yoonjung Kang and earned my Ph.D. in Linguistics at UCLA in 2023, mentored by Professors Bruce Hayes and Sun-Ah Jun.
