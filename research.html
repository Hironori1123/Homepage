Research

Some current projects

  • Loanword accentuation in Japanese: My dissertation work investigates how Japanese speakers assign pitch accent on loanwords.

  • Phonetics and phonology of code-switching/mixing: This project investigates how bilinguals' knowledge is structured by examining code-switched/mixed sentences. We are currently conducting experiments to test if English-Spanish bilinguals apply the English phonological rule of tapping when a language boundary follows the target segment. (in collaboration with Gemma Repiso Puigdelliura and Kie Zuraw)

  • Perception and processing of speech sounds: This project consists of two threads. The first thread examines how intonationally defined prosodic structure modulates perception of vowel length in Japanese. The second thread explores the role of segment and pitch accent in Japanese spoken word recognition. (in collaboration with Jeremy Steffman)

  • Intonational phonology of understudied languages: This project explores the intonational phonology of Ikpana (Logba), an understudied tone language spoken in Ghana.
